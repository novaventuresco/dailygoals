Built on a different belief

Most habit apps are designed to make you feel bad when life happens.

Broken streaks, guilt trips, overdue tasks that pile up — they turn self-improvement into anxiety. Daily Goals is built around one simple idea: each day is its own thing. Yesterday doesn't carry over. You start fresh every morning, no matter what happened the day before.

Daily Focus

What are your top priorities today?

Each morning you start with an empty list. Pick 3–5 things that actually matter today — not a backlog from last week.

Your recent goals are always one tap away, so adding frequently used goals takes seconds.

Progress Tracking

Check off — or track how far you got.

Most apps only let you mark something done or not done. Daily Goals lets you track partial progress: 25%, 50%, 75%, or full completion.

Because "I ran for 15 minutes instead of 30" is still real progress worth acknowledging.

Organisation

Custom categories with colour and icons.

Group related goals under categories you create — Fitness & Health, Work, Personal, whatever fits your life. Each with its own icon and colour.

Or keep it totally flat. The choice is yours.

Weekly Review

Look back without judgment.

The weekly review shows your completion rate, daily breakdown, most productive goal, and patterns over time — presented as context, not a report card.

Streaks are shown quietly if they emerge. They're never used to make you feel bad if they don't.
